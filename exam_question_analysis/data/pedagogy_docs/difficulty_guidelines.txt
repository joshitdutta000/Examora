Guidelines for Assessing and Improving Question Difficulty

1. What Makes a Question Easy?
- Tests recall or basic understanding (Bloom's Level 1-2)
- Uses straightforward language with no ambiguity
- Has a high average student score (avg_score > 7)
- Low standard deviation (std_dev < 1) — most students score similarly
- High discrimination index indicates even easy questions can differentiate

2. What Makes a Question Medium?
- Tests application or analysis (Bloom's Level 3-4)
- Requires students to use knowledge in a new context
- Average student score between 4-7
- Moderate standard deviation (std_dev 1-2)
- Good discrimination index (> 0.3)

3. What Makes a Question Hard?
- Tests evaluation or creation (Bloom's Level 5-6)
- Requires synthesis of multiple concepts
- Low average student score (avg_score < 4)
- High standard deviation (std_dev > 2) — performance varies widely
- May have lower discrimination index if too difficult for most students

4. Improving Easy Questions
- Raise cognitive level from Remember to Apply or Analyze
- Add a problem-solving component
- Introduce a novel context or scenario
- Combine with another concept to increase complexity

5. Improving Hard Questions
- Break into sub-questions to scaffold student thinking
- Provide more context or a worked example before the question
- Reduce ambiguity in wording
- Align with specific taught content rather than inference

6. Improving Medium Questions
- Ensure clear alignment with learning outcomes
- Check discrimination index — if low, rewrite distractors (for MCQ)
- Verify the cognitive level matches the intended difficulty
- Use active voice and precise terminology

7. Learning Gap Indicators
- High std_dev + low avg_score = concept not understood by most students
- Low discrimination index = question does not differentiate learners
- Hard questions on Remember level = likely a wording/clarity issue
- Easy questions on Evaluate level = question may not be testing intended skill
